Building a Climate-Ready Maryland Through Education

The Maryland Outdoor Learning Program (Maryland OLP) works statewide to ensure all students have access to high-quality environment-based learning that prepares them for civic life, careers, and environmental stewardship.

As stewards of the Chesapeake Bay—America's largest estuary—Maryland students gain the knowledge and skills to act responsibly to protect and restore their local watershed.

Maryland's Vision

To enable students to make decisions and take actions that create and maintain an optimal relationship between themselves and the environment, preserving and protecting the unique natural resources of Maryland—particularly those of the Chesapeake Bay and its watershed.

Maryland Environmental Literacy Standards

The Maryland State Board of Education adopted Environmental Literacy Standards in 2011 and revised them in 2020. These standards map out the elements all students must achieve to graduate environmentally literate.

1 Environmental Issues – Analyze issues and take action
2 Interactions of Earth's Systems – Apply systems thinking
3 Flow of Matter and Energy – Understand cycles and flows
4 Populations, Communities & Ecosystems – Analyze interactions
5 Humans and Natural Resources – Examine resource use
6 Environment and Health – Connect environment to wellbeing
7 Environment and Society – Analyze social-environmental links
8 Sustainability – Make decisions for sustainable futures

Meaningful Watershed Educational Experiences

MWEEs are the cornerstone of Maryland's environmental literacy approach—learner-centered experiences that focus on investigations into local environmental issues and lead to informed action.
